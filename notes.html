What a WAV file looks like

Every press release and blog post about wavenet I've come across, have been accompanied by this very helpful gif: It appears to be helpful in conveying, to people with some experience in machine learning but not with audio data, how impressively dense audio data actually is. In particular, it suggests that modeling audio in the time domain, shown here (as opposed to time-frequence domain), might just be really difficult.

This gif is the single most valuable teaching resource I have for explaining how digital audio works. https://t.co/tLj6kr1wnl

— Ethan Hein (@ethanhein) July 10, 2020
